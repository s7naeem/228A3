✿ Education ✿

In high school, my interests were quite diverse, reflecting my curiosity about different subjects. I found enjoyment in both my science and math courses, appreciating the analytical challenges they presented. Simultaneously, my passion for arts and social sciences shone through, with a love for the creativity and insights they offered.

As my high school journey progressed, my focus shifted during my last year. Originally gearing up to apply for a criminology program, I delved into law and social science courses. The allure of exploring the intricate realms of crime and law captivated me, and my interest in forensics and legal studies deepened.

Reflecting on my childhood, I've always been drawn to arts and crafts. This love for creating with my hands and using my imagination persisted into my free time, where I would engage in various projects. From tinkering with ideas inspired by YouTube videos to solving creative challenges, my love for crafting became a constant in my life.

Navigating the transition from high school to university was marked by a period of exploration and self-discovery. While sciences and maths occupied my focus in the initial three years, a significant shift occurred in my final year. Driven by a newfound passion for criminology, I embarked on a two-year journey into the world of crime and law, accompanied by a minor in UX Design.

As much as I cherished my time as a criminology student, the allure of UX design gradually took precedence. After careful contemplation, I made the decision to pivot towards UX design, where I am excited to continue learning and growing within the GBDA program. While I may miss my days as a criminology student, I eagerly anticipate the opportunities for development that lie ahead in the world of UX design.
